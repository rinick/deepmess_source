Tsuro Chess is a board game base on [Tsuro: The Game of the Path](https://calliopegames.com/shop/tsuro-the-game-of-the-path/)

The following rules requires some knowledge of the original Tsuro game.


### Setup

- The game board like the original Tsuro game with 36 squares.
  - The 20 squares next to the edge need to have labels or numbers on them: E1~E4 W1~W4 S1~S4 N1~N4 SE SW NE NW, or simply just 1~20<br/>
- There should also be a D20 dice with labels corresponding to the 20 edge sqaures.
- the 35 path tiles and the dragon tile from the original Tsuro game.
- 2 different colors of pieces are needed, you will need 3 pawn pieces and 1 king piece for each color.
<span style="color:red">♔♙♙♙</span>
<span style="color:blue">♔♙♙♙</span>




To start the game:
1. Lay out the board.
1. Each player choose a color, and decide the order, the order of placing pieces is the same order of playing path tiles.
1. Player 1 first roll the D20 dice, find the square and place the piece on any availible start point within that square. Then player 2 place the same piece on the square on the opposite side, symentrical to the center point.
  - The first square that is not in the corner will be the king piece, others will be pawn pieces.
  - Pieces can not be put on a tile that already has opponent\'s piece, in this case, re-roll the dice.
  - Pieces can be put on a tile that already has your own piece.
1. Keep doing the above step 4 times until all pieces are places on the board.  
1. Shuffle the path tiles and deal 2 tiles to each player, players\' tiles should always be visible to other players.

##### 3 ~ 6 players
 ( Difference from 2 players are in **bold** font )

Each player has his/her own color of 1 king and **2 pawns: ♔♙♙**

To start the game:
1. Lay out the board.
1. Each player choose a color, and decide the order, the order of placing pieces is the same order of playing path tiles. 
1. **Players place pieces on the start marks in turn, until all pieces are places on the board. To do so, the player first roll the D20 dice, find the square and place the piece on any availible start point within that square.**
  - **The player can decide if he wants to place pawn or king after rolling the dice.**
  - Pieces can not be put on a tile that already has opponent\'s piece, in this case, **skip the turn and pass dice to next player**.
  - Pieces can be put on a tile that already has your own piece.
1. Shuffle the path tiles and deal 2 tiles to each player, players\' tiles should always be visible to other players.

### Play the game

Each Turn has three parts:
1. Deal a path tile to player.
2. Player plays a path tile on a open square next to any of the player\'s pieces.
3. Move all the pieces affected by the tile and remove any piece that goes out of board.

##### Winning the game

- When a player\'s king is eliminated, player\'s other pieces will also be eliminated from board. His/her path tile will be given to other players.
- If a player has no other choice but eliminate his/her own king, the player loses immediately.
  - Path tile is not allowed to be played in this case, which also means the player\'s remaining pieces are directly removed from board and other player\'s pieces are not affected during this turn.
- When only one king piece remains on the board, that player wins the game.
- If more than one king remains after all 35 tiles are played, it\'s a draw.
  - In case there must be a winner, the remaining player that plays the last path tile wins.
